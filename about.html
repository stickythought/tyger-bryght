About this site

This website was created to carry on traditions of the indie web including unique page themes, decentralized community, rudimentary knowledge of HTML, and poor mobile optimization. You'll find all of that in the source code which can be used with or without attribution. I am still learning a lot but I've tried to make it easy to follow.

The site name comes from William Blake's poem "The Tyger", which questions the contradictions of creation.

About me

I love reading, space, human history, and technology. I'm most drawn to cyber ethics, law, organization, and have an associate's degree in cyber security. Some favorite projects I've been building are genealogical data-conversion tools and satellite data-analysis tools.
